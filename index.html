What your BMI result means

A BMI range of 18.5 to 24.9 is considered a healthy weight. Maintaining a healthy weight may lower your chances of experiences health issues later on, such as obesity and type 2 diabetes. Aim for nutritious diet with reduced fat and sugar content, incorporating ample fruits and vegetables. Additonally, strive for regular physical activity, ideally about 30 minuites daily for five days a week.

Healthy eating

Healthy eating prormotes weight control,disease prevention, better digestion, immunity, mental clarity and mood.

Regular exercise

Exercise improves fitness, aids wieght control, elevates mood, and reduces disease risk, fostering wellness and longevity.

Adequate sleep

Sleep enhances mental clarity, emotional stability, and physical wellness, promoting overall restoration and rejuvenation.

Limitations of BMI

Although BMI is often a prectical indicator of healthy weight, it is not suitedfor every person. Specific groups should carefully consider their BMI outcomes, and in certain cases, the measurement may not be beneficial to use.

Gender

The development and body fat composition of girls and boys vary with age. Consequently, a child's age and gender are considered when evaluating their BMI.

Age

In aging individuals, increased body fat and muscle loss may cause BMI to underestimate body fat content.

Muscle

BMI mat misclassify mascluar individuals as overweight or obese, as it doesn't differentiate muscle from fat.

Pregenancy

Expectant mothers experience weight gain due to their growing baby. Maintaining a healthy pre-pregenancy BMI is advisable to minimise health risks for both mother amd child.

Race

Certain health concerns may affect individuals of some Black and Asian origins at lower BMI tha others. To learn more, it is advised to discuss this with your GP or practice nurse.
